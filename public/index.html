Cookies

Text files with small pieces of data that are used to identify your computer as you use a computer network. HTTP cookies are used to identify specific users and improve your web browsing experience. While first-party cookies are directly created and used by the website you are using, third-party cookies are generated by websites that are different from the web pages users are currently surfing, and lets advertisers or analytics companies track an individual’s browsing history across the web on any sites that contain their ads.

Learn more

Fingerprinting

Specific information collected about a remote computing device forthe purpose of identification. Fingerprints can be used to uniquely identify individual users or devices even when cookies are turned off. Websites use fingerprinting to collect information about your browser type and version, as well as your timezone, language, screen resolution, and various other active settings, all without user consent. This information is valuable for advertising purposes, and can be used to group users together to target certain groups in different ways.

So how can you protect your information? With so many common digital tools aggressively seeking out your information wherever possible, the only way to ensure the privacy of your data is through proactive means. Here are a few strategies to start taking action...

VPN - Virtual Private Network

    Hides your IP address by letting the network redirect it through a specifically configured remote server run by a VPN host

  • Works like a filter that turns all your data into "gibberish"
  • Disguises your data traffic online and protects it from external access
Learn more

Tip: VPNs give users the ability to move their IP geo-location to different parts of the world. If a user were to set their IP geo-location to that of the European Union, they can be protected under the GDPR privacy requirements.

Brave Browser

    Fast, private, and secure web browser for PC, Mac, and Mobile

  • Blocks all ads and trackers by default
  • Fingerprint randomization
    • Intentionally makes you look different to every site, each time you restart the browser
    • Prevents sites from using fingerprinting to track you across sites or across sessions
  • Blocks all third-party storage
Learn more

Tor Browser

    Uses Onion Routing, a technique for anonymous communication over a computer network where message are encapsulated in layers of encryption

  • Traffic is relayed and encrypted three times as it passes over the tor network
  • Blocks trackers by isolating each website you visit so third-party trackers and ads can't follow you
  • Any cookies automatically clear when you're done browsing
  • Defends against surveilance by preventing anyone from watching your connection and knowing what websites your visit. All anyone monitoring your browsing habits can see is that you're using Tor
  • Resist Fingerprinting by making all users look the same, making it difficult for you to be fingerprinted based on your browser and device info
Learn more

Security Plugins

Dynamically loadable library that provides authentication security services.

  • Designed to block scripts that potentially enable spying ads and invisible trackers from running in your browser
  • Blocks advertisers and other third-party tracking software from tracking your online activities
